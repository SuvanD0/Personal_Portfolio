# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters and people considering hiring Suvan Dommeti. They arrive having already seen a LinkedIn profile or resume and want to understand who he actually is: his goals, personality, and how he thinks. The job is to give them something LinkedIn cannot.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Suvan Dommeti. Showcases work experience, projects, and the data he tracks about his own life. Success means a recruiter leaves with a clear, specific sense of who Suvan is as a person and professional — not just a list of credentials.

## Brand Personality

Builder, curious, data-driven. The voice is direct and confident without being stiff. Personality comes through the content itself (what he tracks, what he makes, what he cares about) rather than through decorative flourish or adjective-heavy copy.

## References

- dvdrod.com — understated and substance-first; skills and personality integrated naturally into the layout rather than listed dryly; quantified outcomes; humanizing without trying too hard
- claude.ai — generous whitespace, high contrast, modern and clean, nothing decorative without purpose

## Anti-references

Generic portfolio patterns: hero sections with a tagline and floating icons, identical skill-badge grids, card walls with icon + heading + paragraph repeated endlessly, gradient text, glassmorphism as default aesthetic.

## Design Principles

1. **Beyond the resume.** Surface what LinkedIn and a PDF cannot: interests, habits, real data, taste. Every page should add dimension that credentials alone cannot.
2. **Data as personality.** Suvan tracks things. That is itself a character trait. Stats pages and health data are not novelties — they are part of who he is. Display them with the same care as work experience.
3. **Show, don't claim.** Avoid adjectives about yourself. Let the work, the data, and the specificity do the claiming.
4. **Purposeful restraint.** The aesthetic is clean and modern (think Claude, dvdrod). Nothing is there to fill space. Every element earns its place.
5. **Specificity over generality.** Vague copy ("passionate about technology") is invisible. Specific copy ("currently building X", "last listened to Y", "averaged Z hours of sleep") is memorable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as a reasonable baseline. Sufficient color contrast in both light and dark modes. No accessibility-specific user needs beyond defaults.
